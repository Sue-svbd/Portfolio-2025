My design process

1.
DISCOVERY & DIAGNOSIS: UNDERSTANDING THE SYSTEM

I start by digging deep to figure out the real problem. I do user research and use tools like Hotjar to see exactly how people are behaving, which gives me concrete data on their frustrations and where we can improve.

2.
ARCHITECTURE & IDEATION: STRUCTURING THE SOLUTION

Once I have a solid grasp on the problem, I start mapping out the solution. I begin with simple wireframes and user flows to figure out the most straightforward path for a person to take. At this early stage, it's all about getting the core experience right—the visual design can wait.

3.
PROTOTYPING & VALIDATION: MAKING IDEAS INTERACTIVE

Next, I turn those sketches into interactive prototypes. Because I can code, I often build these as high-fidelity mockups or even small, functional websites. This gives the team and our test users something realistic to click through, which leads to much better feedback. We’ll discuss, test, and refine the flow together until it feels completely intuitive.

4.
EXECUTION & ITERATION: CRAFTING THE EXPERIENCE

With the user experience locked in, I focus on designing the final, polished UI. My goal is always to create a look that's clean, elegant, and feels human. From there, I work with the developers to make sure the final product is built exactly as designed. The work doesn't stop at launch, though as I'm always watching user feedback and performance data to figure out what we can improve in the next cycle.

My design philosophy

I believe the best products are born from a blend of sharp logic and creative intuition. My process starts by digging deep into the system, the users, and what they need to accomplish. I map out the complexities to build a logical foundation, and from that structure, I explore creative solutions that feel simple, elegant, and helpful. At the end of the day, my job is to turn complexity into clarity, and data into an experience that people genuinely enjoy using.
